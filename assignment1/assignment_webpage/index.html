Interactive Media Assignment 1 ❄️

Q1

The first thing I noticed was that the website felt difficult to understand at first glance. Unlike a typical webpage with obvious navigation features, this one did not provide clear directions. Because of this, I felt curious and began to explore through interaction.

Q2

I scrolled down the webpage first, because the unusual layout did not present a clear path. Unlike conventional single-scrolling websites, this one had two different sections of content on the same page. I noticed hints from the images on the site, and some icons began to appear. I clicked on them to see what would happen. I discovered hidden 3D elements inside the advent calendar. I clicked more frequently to uncover all the hidden elements. I clicked across the calendar grid and realized not every box contained hidden elements. After ensuring I had explored all the advent calendar boxes, I moved to the right side of the webpage to expect further features. I clicked on each project displayed on the site. I returned to the main page and noticed that all previously discovered hidden elements had reset in the calendar grid.

Q3

I spent most of my time discovering the Christmas icons hidden inside the advent calendar, which made the experience playful and surprising.

Q4

The most common action I performed was clicking repeatedly to uncover hidden features and check if anything new could be revealed.

Q5

I believe the primary goal is to evoke the user's curiosity and encourage self-directed navigation. This method holds the user's attention by creating unexpected discoveries, allowing them to absorb the content unconsciously while being entertained.

Q6

The experience communicates its goal by leaving space for imagination. As users interact, they are rewarded with charming elements that make the process engaging and immersive. This playful design keeps the user involved and motivates them to continue exploring.

Q7

The experience feels designed for short bursts of engagement around 1–2 minutes at a time and could be revisited 2–3 times for renewed enjoyment.

Q8

It communicates this through its random and unstructured interactive features, which suggest that the site is not meant for a single linear visit but rather for repeated, playful exploration.

Q9

The experience references advent calendars, interactive posters, and playful web art installations.

Q10

It suggests that as a user, I should not expect straightforward information delivery. Instead, I should approach the site playfully, allowing curiosity to guide me. This strategy reflects the importance of using design to sustain attention while balancing aesthetics and storytelling.

Q11

It communicates that I should feel curious and excited, experiencing a sense of wonder as I uncover hidden surprises.

Q12

The most frustrating part was the landing page layout. At first, I did not know what to do, which could easily confuse or discourage impatient users. An improvement would be to include a small hint guiding the user to click on the calendar first instead of randomly clicking around.

Q13

The most satisfying part was the anticipation of opening each advent calendar box. Each revisit reset the grid to its original state, allowing me to re-explore as if it were new. I particularly enjoyed discovering Christmas icons with hidden text underneath. When clicked, the background color sometimes changed to red, creating a delightful "double-layered" surprise I did not expect.
